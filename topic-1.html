Early Days

The desktop web began with early graphical browsers like Mosaic (1993), which popularized inline images and a more accessible interface. Netscape Navigator soon followed, leading to rapid adoption and competition.

Browser Wars

  • Netscape Navigator — early market leader with rapid features.
  • Internet Explorer — bundled with Windows; dominant by early 2000s.
  • Opera — innovative features (tabs, speed dial) with a smaller user base.
  • Mozilla Firefox — open-source alternative with standards and extensions.
  • Safari (macOS) — introduced WebKit and performance improvements.

Standards & Engines

Over time, standards from the W3C and WHATWG guided interoperability. Engines evolved: Trident (IE), Gecko (Firefox), WebKit (Safari), and Blink (Chrome).
